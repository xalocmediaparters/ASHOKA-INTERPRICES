Central Financial Assistance (CFA) for Rooftop Solar: PM Surya Ghar: Muft Bijli Yojana

The PM Surya Ghar: Muft Bijli Yojana is the flagship Central Government scheme, administered by the Ministry of New and Renewable Energy (MNRE), designed to make solar energy affordable and accessible for residential consumers across India.

This scheme provides a substantial Central Financial Assistance (CFA) or subsidy to reduce the upfront cost of installing a grid-connected Rooftop Solar (RTS) system, empowering households to generate their own electricity and achieve significant savings on their monthly bills.

1. How the Subsidy Works: Simplified Process

The entire process is managed transparently through the National Portal for Rooftop Solar, ensuring the subsidy is directly transferred to the consumer's bank account (DBT).

Step Description
1 Apply Online: Register and apply on the official National Portal using your mobile number and electricity consumer account details.
2 Get Approval: Receive technical feasibility approval from your local Electricity Distribution Company (DISCOM).
3 Choose a Vendor: Select a solar vendor from the list of MNRE-registered/empanelled companies for installation.
4 Installation & Net Metering: The vendor installs the grid-connected solar system and assists with the Net Meter application.
5 Inspection & Commissioning: The DISCOM inspects the system, installs the Net Meter, and issues the commissioning certificate.
6 Receive Subsidy: Upload your bank details on the portal. The fixed subsidy amount is transferred directly to your bank account within the stipulated time.

3. Eligibility and Key Conditions

To successfully claim the MNRE subsidy, applicants must meet the following mandatory criteria:

  • Residential Status: The scheme is exclusively for Residential Consumers (individual households).
  • Grid-Connected System: The solar power plant must be a Grid-Connected system (requires a Net Meter). Off-grid systems are not eligible for this CFA.
  • MNRE-Empanelled Vendor: The installation must be carried out by a vendor registered under the National Portal/DISCOM empanelment.
  • Quality Compliance: The solar modules (panels) and inverters used must be listed in the Approved List of Models and Manufacturers (ALMM) and meet all MNRE quality standards.
  • No Prior Subsidy: The household must not have previously received a subsidy for a rooftop solar system under any other Central or State government scheme.
  • Application Pre-Installation: The subsidy application must be submitted and approved before the installation commences.
